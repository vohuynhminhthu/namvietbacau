Six mistakes when eating
fresh mushroom

1 / Wash mushrooms thoroughly

Mushrooms contain Lysergic, a substance that can be converted into sunlight into Vitamin D. Mushrooms if washed off or soaked in water for a long time can cause loss of nutrients in fresh mushrooms.


2 / Cook the mushrooms in an aluminum pot

Mushrooms when cooked in aluminum pots will discolor very poorly, being careful not to use pots with this material to cook dishes from mushrooms.


3 / Cook with too much cooking oil

Mushrooms suck water and other liquids, so too much oil will interfere with the absorption of nutrients from mushrooms, cause bloating, affect digestion, and even risk gastroesophageal reflux.


4 / Cook mushrooms under low heat

When processing mushrooms, you should set the temperature at high temperatures because if left at a low temperature, the mushrooms will produce lots of water, crushed, mushy and bland to retain the best flavor and color.


5 / Not fully cooked

It is necessary to boil the mushrooms for 5-10 minutes to make sure the mushrooms are fully cooked to ensure hygiene and not harmful to your body.


6 / Eat mushrooms with cold drinks

Mushrooms are welding and tonic if you drink cold food, you will have a cold stomach, prone to abdominal pain, discomfort, harmful to health.

Mushrooms have antioxidant reserves equivalent to the healthiest products of any vegetable. The mushroom's selenium inhibits tumor growth, fights inflammation, activates liver enzymes and aids in neutralizing certain carcinogenic elements.
